Growth Equity Investing: Opportunities in High-Growth Companies by Jack Estes DeBrabander

Back to All Articles

By Jack Estes DeBrabander | Published February 12, 2026

Growth equity occupies a distinctive and increasingly important position in the private investment landscape. Situated between early-stage venture capital and late-stage buyout investing, growth equity targets companies that have moved beyond the startup phase, achieved product-market fit, and are generating meaningful revenue, but still possess substantial runway for expansion. These are businesses that have proven their core value proposition and are now seeking capital to scale operations, enter new markets, and accelerate their path toward market leadership. Jack Estes DeBrabander has identified growth equity as one of the most compelling opportunities in private markets, offering a combination of reduced early-stage risk and significant upside potential that appeals to a broad range of accredited investors.

In this article, Jack Estes DeBrabander provides a comprehensive guide to growth equity investing, covering the characteristics of ideal target companies, the sectors that present the most compelling opportunities, and the analytical framework used to identify and evaluate growth equity investments. For accredited investors seeking exposure to the private markets, growth equity represents a strategic entry point that balances the pursuit of attractive returns with a more favorable risk profile than earlier-stage venture investing.

What Is Growth Equity?

Growth equity is a category of private investment focused on providing expansion capital to companies that are in the scaling phase of their lifecycle. Unlike venture capital, which funds companies at the earliest stages when the business model may still be unproven, growth equity targets companies that have already demonstrated viability through consistent revenue growth, a growing customer base, and a product or service that has gained meaningful market traction. The capital provided through growth equity investments is typically used to accelerate organic growth rather than to fund research and development or prove basic product concepts.

The defining characteristics of growth equity investments include minority ownership positions, meaning the investor typically acquires less than a controlling stake in the company. Growth equity investors generally do not seek to restructure the business or replace management; instead, they provide capital and strategic support to help the existing team execute on an established growth plan. This collaborative approach distinguishes growth equity from traditional private equity buyouts, where investors acquire controlling interests and often implement significant operational changes.

Jack Estes DeBrabander focuses on growth equity opportunities because they sit at what many consider the optimal point on the private market risk-return spectrum. The companies involved have de-risked the fundamental business model questions that make venture capital inherently uncertain, while still retaining the kind of growth trajectory that can generate multiples of invested capital over a reasonable holding period. For accredited investors who want private market exposure without the binary risk profile of early-stage ventures, growth equity offers a compelling middle ground.

Target Company Profiles

Not every growing company is an appropriate growth equity investment. The best growth equity opportunities share a specific set of characteristics that distinguish them from both earlier-stage companies that are still proving their business models and later-stage companies that have already captured the majority of their addressable market. Jack Estes DeBrabander evaluates potential growth equity investments against a detailed profile that encompasses several critical dimensions.

Revenue scale and trajectory are foundational criteria. Ideal growth equity targets typically generate between $10 million and $200 million in annual revenue and are growing at rates of 20 percent or more year over year. This combination of scale and growth velocity indicates a company that has achieved meaningful market adoption and is positioned for continued expansion. Companies at this stage have typically moved beyond the phase where a single lost customer or failed product launch could fundamentally threaten the business.

Unit economics provide critical insight into the sustainability of growth. Jack Estes DeBrabander examines metrics such as customer acquisition cost, lifetime value of a customer, gross margin, and net revenue retention to determine whether the company's growth is economically viable. A company that is growing rapidly but spending more to acquire each customer than that customer will ever generate in revenue is not a sound growth equity investment, regardless of how impressive the top-line numbers may appear.

Market position and competitive advantage are equally important. The best growth equity candidates occupy defensible positions in large, growing markets. They may benefit from network effects, proprietary technology, regulatory advantages, high switching costs, or strong brand recognition that creates barriers to competitive entry. Jack Estes DeBrabander assesses the durability of these advantages to determine whether the company can sustain its growth trajectory over the investment horizon.

Technology Sector Opportunities

The technology sector has been the largest and most productive hunting ground for growth equity investments, and it continues to present compelling opportunities across multiple sub-sectors. Software companies, in particular, have characteristics that make them especially well-suited to growth equity investing. Their recurring revenue models, high gross margins, and scalable delivery mechanisms create the kind of predictable, high-quality revenue growth that growth equity investors prize.

Enterprise software companies that sell to businesses represent a particularly attractive segment. These companies typically benefit from long-term contracts, high switching costs, and the ability to expand revenue within existing customers through upselling and cross-selling. Jack Estes DeBrabander evaluates enterprise software opportunities by examining net revenue retention rates, which measure the company's ability to grow revenue from its existing customer base. Companies with net revenue retention above 120 percent are effectively growing even without acquiring new customers, which is a powerful indicator of product value and competitive strength.

Cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, cloud infrastructure, and data analytics are among the technology sub-sectors that Jack Estes DeBrabander monitors most closely for growth equity opportunities. Each of these areas benefits from secular tailwinds that are driving sustained demand growth, creating favorable conditions for well-positioned companies to capture significant market share. The key is identifying companies within these sectors that have demonstrated product-market fit and are ready to scale aggressively with the right capital infusion.

Healthcare Sector Opportunities

Healthcare represents another sector where growth equity investing can generate exceptional returns, driven by a combination of demographic trends, regulatory evolution, and technological innovation. The healthcare industry is characterized by large addressable markets, high barriers to entry, and a fundamental demand profile that is largely independent of economic cycles. Jack Estes DeBrabander identifies healthcare growth equity opportunities across several distinct sub-sectors.

Health technology companies that leverage software and data analytics to improve clinical outcomes, reduce costs, or enhance patient experience have emerged as a particularly dynamic area. Telemedicine platforms, electronic health record systems, clinical decision support tools, and remote patient monitoring solutions are all experiencing rapid adoption driven by healthcare providers' need to improve efficiency and outcomes while managing costs. Growth equity investments in these companies benefit from the combination of large market opportunity and the recurring revenue characteristics typical of software businesses.

Healthcare services companies, including specialty medical practices, diagnostic laboratories, and outpatient treatment centers, also present compelling growth equity opportunities. These businesses typically grow through a combination of organic expansion and strategic acquisitions, building scale that drives operational efficiency and negotiating leverage with payers. Jack Estes DeBrabander evaluates these opportunities by examining same-store growth metrics, acquisition integration capabilities, and the sustainability of the company's competitive advantages in its specific healthcare niche.

Fintech Sector Opportunities

Financial technology, or fintech, is among the most dynamic sectors for growth equity investing. The convergence of mobile technology, data analytics, and changing consumer expectations has created opportunities for innovative companies to challenge traditional financial services incumbents across payments, lending, insurance, wealth management, and banking. Jack Estes DeBrabander has identified fintech as a sector with particularly strong structural tailwinds that support sustained growth for well-positioned companies.

Payment processing and infrastructure companies are a major area of focus. The ongoing shift from cash to digital payments, the growth of e-commerce, and the increasing complexity of cross-border transactions are creating enormous demand for companies that can provide fast, secure, and cost-effective payment solutions. Growth equity investments in payment companies benefit from transaction-based revenue models that scale directly with the volume of economic activity processed through their platforms.

Lending technology companies that use data analytics and machine learning to improve credit decisioning and streamline the borrowing experience represent another compelling opportunity. These companies can often underwrite credit more accurately and efficiently than traditional lenders, resulting in better outcomes for both borrowers and investors. Jack Estes DeBrabander evaluates fintech lending opportunities by examining the quality of the company's credit models, the performance of its loan portfolio, and the regulatory environment in which it operates.

How Jack Estes DeBrabander Identifies Growth Equity Opportunities

Identifying exceptional growth equity investments requires a combination of broad market awareness, deep sector expertise, and disciplined analytical rigor. Jack Estes DeBrabander employs a systematic approach to deal sourcing and evaluation that is designed to surface the highest-quality opportunities while filtering out those that do not meet the rigorous standards required for presentation to the investor network.

The process begins with proactive sector monitoring. Jack Estes DeBrabander tracks industry trends, analyzes market data, and maintains relationships with company founders, venture capital firms, investment banks, and other participants in the private market ecosystem. This network-driven approach ensures that compelling opportunities are identified early, often before they are broadly marketed to the investor community.

When a potential growth equity opportunity is identified, it enters the formal due diligence process. This includes detailed financial analysis to validate the company's revenue quality, growth sustainability, and path to profitability. Management assessment evaluates whether the leadership team has the experience and capabilities needed to execute the growth plan. Market analysis confirms that the company is operating in a large, growing market with a defensible competitive position. And structural review ensures that the terms of the investment are fair and provide appropriate protections for investors.

Jack Estes DeBrabander believes that the combination of sector expertise, rigorous analysis, and a broad network of deal flow sources creates a meaningful advantage in identifying growth equity investments that are positioned to deliver strong returns. For accredited investors seeking to build a diversified private market portfolio, growth equity represents a strategic allocation that combines the potential for significant capital appreciation with a more favorable risk profile than many other areas of private investing.
